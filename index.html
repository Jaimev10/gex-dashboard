1. What is Gamma Exposure (GEX)?

GEX measures how options dealers (market makers) are positioned and how they must hedge their books.

📊 The Big Idea

When you buy options, dealers sell them and hedge with stock. As price moves, they re-hedge — buying or selling shares. Their hedging activity moves the market.

Two Regimes That Change Everything:

🟢 POSITIVE GAMMA
Mean-Reverting Market
  • Dealers buy dips, sell rips
  • Volatility gets SUPPRESSED
  • Price chops around magnet
  • Selling premium works
  • Range-bound day expected
🔴 NEGATIVE GAMMA
Trending Market
  • Dealers sell dips, buy rips
  • Volatility gets AMPLIFIED
  • Price moves AWAY from levels
  • Buying premium works
  • Trend day expected

2. Color Guide — Read Every Marker

Each color and symbol on the ladder tells you something specific. Master these and you can read the dashboard at a glance.

⚪ Spot Price (White Box)

What it is: The current market price, highlighted with a white background box around the strike number.

Why it matters: All other levels are read relative to this. Distance from spot tells you how relevant a level is RIGHT NOW.

How to use: Focus on levels within 1-2% of spot for short-term trading. Far levels matter for the bigger picture.

🧲

🧲 Gamma Magnet (Gold Gradient)

What it is: The strike where price is being PULLED toward. Calculated as the highest |GEX| strike weighted by concentration and distance.

Strength stars (1-5):

  • ★★★★★ Dominant Pin: Price will get magnetized strongly — sell premium here
  • ★★★★ Strong Pin: Reliable target — high probability of price returning
  • ★★★ Moderate Pull: Magnet exists but not dominant — use as one of many factors
  • ★★ Weak Pull: Marginal magnet — watch walls more than this
  • ★ No Pin: Negative gamma regime — magnets don't work, market trends

How to trade it:

✅ POSITIVE GAMMA + STRONG PIN

Sell straddles/strangles at the magnet. Sell call spreads above, put spreads below. Profit from time decay as price chops.

⚠️ NEGATIVE GAMMA

Magnet is unreliable. Price will likely trend AWAY. Buy options, ride momentum.

🟡 Call Wall (Yellow Row)

What it is: The strongest resistance level above spot. Where dealers have heavy positive gamma — they SELL into rallies to hedge.

Why it works as resistance: If price rises toward the call wall, dealers must sell more stock to stay hedged, pushing price back down.

How to trade it:

  • Approach with caution: Expect price to stall or reverse here
  • Short call spreads: Sell calls AT or ABOVE call wall, buy further OTM
  • Long puts/put debit spreads: If price reaches wall in positive gamma
  • Breakout warning: If price BREAKS above call wall, dealers chase = explosive move higher

✨ Resistance Zone (Gold Border)

What it is: Cluster of strikes near the call wall with significant gamma. Indicates a "zone" of resistance, not just one level.

Why it matters: Stronger than a single strike. Price often gets rejected anywhere in this zone, not exactly AT the wall.

How to trade it:

  • Don't expect a clean rejection AT the call wall — anywhere in the zone counts
  • Use the wider zone for stop placement
  • Multiple strikes = stronger conviction

🟣 Put Wall (Purple Row)

What it is: The strongest support level below spot. Where dealers have heavy negative gamma — they BUY into dips to hedge.

Why it works as support: If price falls toward put wall, dealers buy stock to hedge, pushing price back up.

How to trade it:

  • Buy the dip: Long calls or call spreads when price approaches put wall
  • Cash-secured puts: Sell puts AT or BELOW the wall
  • Bullish put spreads: Sell put AT wall, buy further OTM
  • Breakdown warning: If price BREAKS below put wall, dealers chase = waterfall move lower

✨ Support Zone (Purple Border)

What it is: Cluster of strikes near the put wall — a SUPPORT zone, not just a single level.

How to trade it: Use the zone for entries on bullish trades and stop placement.

🟢 Positive GEX (Green Tint)

What it is: Strikes where dealers are LONG gamma (positive net GEX).

What it means: Dealers SELL into rallies at these strikes — they act as a brake on upside moves.

Reading the intensity: Brighter green = larger GEX magnitude = stronger braking effect.

🔴 Negative GEX (Red Tint)

What it is: Strikes where dealers are SHORT gamma (negative net GEX).

What it means: Dealers BUY into rallies at these strikes — they AMPLIFY moves rather than dampen them.

Warning: When the entire market regime turns negative (Net GEX < 0), the magnet doesn't work and walls fail more often. Trend days happen.

+%

🏷️ OI Change Badges

What it is: A badge appears on strikes with significant Open Interest changes from yesterday.

Green +N: NEW positions being built — fresh activity at this strike

Red -N: Positions being CLOSED — dealers unwinding

How to use it: Fresh OI = stronger wall. A call wall with a green +50% badge is much stronger than a stale one.

4. Trading Strategies by Regime

📈 Positive Gamma Strategies

Strategy 1: Pin Sale (Most Common)

Setup: Strong magnet (★★★★+) within 1% of spot, positive net GEX.

Trade: Sell at-the-money iron condor or strangle expiring same day.

Why it works: Theta decay accelerates as price chops around magnet.

Risk: Regime change to negative gamma during the day.

Strategy 2: Range Fade

Setup: Price near call wall (resistance) with positive gamma.

Trade: Sell short-dated call spread above wall.

Why it works: Dealer hedging pushes price back toward magnet.

Strategy 3: Buy the Dip

Setup: Price near put wall with strong support zone.

Trade: Sell put spread or buy calls.

Why it works: Dealer buying creates a floor.

📉 Negative Gamma Strategies

Strategy 1: Momentum Ride

Setup: Net GEX negative, price breaking key level.

Trade: Buy directional options in the direction of break.

Why it works: Dealers must chase moves, amplifying trend.

Strategy 2: Volatility Long

Setup: Negative regime, no clear magnet.

Trade: Long straddle or VIX calls.

Why it works: Volatility tends to expand in negative gamma.

Strategy 3: Breakout Hunt

Setup: Price approaches call wall or put wall in negative regime.

Trade: Buy directional in direction of approach.

Why it works: Walls often FAIL in negative gamma → explosive moves.

5. Common Patterns to Recognize

🏔️ "Walking the Wall"

Price slowly creeps up the call wall throughout the day, getting rejected at slightly higher levels. Strong positive gamma. Trade: Sell call spreads, let theta work.

🧲 "Pin Day"

Price spends entire session within 0.5% of the magnet. Dominant gamma magnet, positive regime. Trade: Iron condor centered on magnet.

💥 "Wall Break"

Price breaks above call wall (or below put wall) with conviction. Dealers must scramble to hedge. Trade: Buy directional options aggressively in direction of break.

🌊 "Squeeze Setup"

Price is between two strong walls in tight range. Volatility crushing. Trade: Iron condor or wait for the break.

🪓 "Trend Day"

Net GEX negative, price trending hard in one direction. Walls keep failing. Trade: Stay with trend, buy options, no fading.

6. Risk Management Rules

1
Never trade against the regime. If net GEX is negative, don't sell premium. If positive, don't chase momentum.
2
Watch for regime flips. Net GEX can flip from positive to negative mid-day. Adjust your strategy immediately.
3
Use confidence stars. ★★ pin is much weaker than ★★★★★. Size positions based on conviction.
4
0DTE has the strongest gamma effects. Pin precision is highest. Longer DTEs are softer.
5
Levels can shift intraday. Refresh frequently. New flow changes walls and magnets.
6
Macro events override gamma. FOMC, CPI, earnings → gamma signals weaken. Trade smaller or stay out.
